Site Location

Your site's latitude and longitude are used to calculate the sun's path across the sky throughout the year. The calculator uses the winter solstice (December 21) — the day the sun is lowest in the sky and shadows are longest — to give you the worst-case spacing requirement.

Panel or Array Dimensions

The physical size and tilt of your panels determine how long a shadow each row casts onto the row behind it. Enter the dimensions of a single panel if calculating spacing between individual panel rows, or the combined array height if multiple panel rows are mounted together as a single block.

Slope length is measured along the face of the panel — not the horizontal ground distance. For a standard 60-cell panel this is typically around 1.65 m; for a 72-cell panel around 1.96 m. If your array stacks two portrait panels high, double the single-panel slope length.

Land Use Optimisation (optional)

Tighter row spacing means more panels fit on a given area — but once any shadow reaches a panel, a bypass diode activates and an entire section of that panel drops to zero output. Because bypass diodes protect equal-height bands, there is no extra power penalty for increasing shading within a band once that diode has already tripped. This creates spacing "sweet spots" worth finding.

Enter your cost inputs below. The calculator will sweep shading tolerance from 0% to the point before a second full bypass section is lost, and show the cost per effective kWp at every step.
